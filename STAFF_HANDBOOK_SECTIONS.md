# Modular Staff Handbook Sections: Professional Standards

These sections are designed to be copied directly into a larger Employee Handbook or used as standalone training modules.

---

## SECTION: UNIFORM AND GROOMING (THE BRAND STANDARD)
**Policy Statement:** Our staff members are the face of [Venue Name]. Your appearance should reflect the elegance and professionalism of the events we host.

*   **Uniform Requirements:** [Insert specific uniform items, e.g., Solid black button-down shirt, black dress slacks, and black non-slip dress shoes].
*   **Maintenance:** Employees are responsible for ensuring their uniform is clean, pressed, and in good repair.
*   **Jewelry and Fragrance:** Keep jewelry minimal and professional. Avoid heavy scents that may be distracting or cause allergic reactions for guests.
*   **Electronics:** Personal electronic devices, including smartwatches and wireless earbuds, should not be visible or used during service hours.

---

## SECTION: SERVICE EXCELLENCE (THE INVISIBLE STANDARD)
**Policy Statement:** We strive for seamless, unobtrusive service that anticipates guest needs without being intrusive.

*   **Guest First:** Guests always have the right of way. Move aside and offer a polite greeting as they pass.
*   **Communication:** When a guest has a request, respond with confidence. If you don't know an answer, reassure them: "I will find out for you immediately," and follow through.
*   **Personal Conduct:** Maintain a professional "on-stage" presence. Personal conversations between staff should be kept to a minimum and away from guest areas.
*   **Personal Belongings:** Keep the floor clear of personal items. Use designated lockers for bags and phones.

---

## SECTION: PROFESSIONAL BOUNDARIES AND ALCOHOL
**Policy Statement:** Professionalism is maintained through clear boundaries between staff and guests.

*   **Fraternization:** Interaction with guests should be professional and service-oriented. Do not engage in social activities (dancing, drinking, or personal photography) during the event.
*   **Alcohol Policy:**
    *   No staff member is permitted to consume alcohol while on the clock or on venue property.
    *   **Bartenders:** You are responsible for the safe and legal service of alcohol. You must be familiar with and follow all local and state (Indiana ATC) regulations. Over-serving or unauthorized "heavy pours" are grounds for immediate dismissal.

---

## SECTION: PRIVACY AND SOCIAL MEDIA
**Policy Statement:** We protect the "inner circle" of our clients' private celebrations.

*   **Confidentiality:** Information regarding event details, guest lists, or private conversations overheard during an event must remain confidential.
*   **Social Media:** Do not capture or share content from an event on personal accounts. This includes "behind the scenes" shots of decor or the wedding party. All official venue social media is handled by the Marketing Department or Venue Manager.
