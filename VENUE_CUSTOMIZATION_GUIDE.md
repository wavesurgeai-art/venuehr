# Venue Customization Guide: HR Document Templates

These templates are designed to provide a professional foundation for your venue's HR operations. To get the most out of them, follow these customization steps.

## 1. Brand Alignment
While the templates use standard professional language, your venue likely has its own unique "voice."
*   **The "Look":** The provided uniform (black button-down, black slacks) is a standard. If your venue uses vests, aprons, or a different color palette (e.g., navy blue or white), update the `STAFF_AGREEMENT_TEMPLATE.md` and `STAFF_HANDBOOK_SECTIONS.md` accordingly.
*   **Logo and Letterhead:** When moving these templates into a Word document or PDF, ensure you include your venue's logo and official contact information at the top.

## 2. Legal Compliance
*   **Local Laws:** The templates mention Indiana ATC guidelines for alcohol service. If your venue is outside of Indiana, ensure you update this to the relevant state or local regulatory body (e.g., TABC in Texas, ABC in California).
*   **Employment Status:** These templates assume an At-Will employment relationship (common in many US states). If you have specific union requirements or different contract types, consult with legal counsel to adjust the acknowledgment language.

## 3. Operations & Facilities
*   **Lockers vs. Vehicles:** If you do not have staff lockers, update the cell phone policy to specify where phones should be kept (e.g., "in personal vehicles or designated staff bins").
*   **Drinking/Smoking Areas:** Be specific in your training about where the "out of view" areas are located. Update the `ONBOARDING_CHECKLIST.md` to include these specific locations.

## 4. Social Media Exceptions
*   **Marketing Team:** If you have specific staff members authorized to take photos for the venue's official Instagram/TikTok, clarify that the social media ban applies to *personal* accounts and *unauthorized* capture.

## 5. Implementation Strategy
1.  **Review:** Have your Venue Manager or Ownership review the drafted language.
2.  **Format:** Move the Markdown text into your preferred document editor (Word, Google Docs, etc.).
3.  **Distribute:** Include these documents in your hiring packets.
4.  **Enforce:** Consistency is key. Ensure Lead Coordinators are trained to uphold these standards during every event.
